Find research participants

Participants are the people who take part in user research. For research to be effective, participants must be actual or likely users of the service.

Recruit internal participants

Use staff directories to find colleagues in the Ministry of Defence (MOD).

For longer research campaigns, you should engage through the existing Chain of Command (CoC) and tasking processes.

If you cannot find a specific group to research with, you can speak to Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) and supporting military personnel to help you with recruitment.

Large scale testing

There may be existing processes in place for large scale testing activity. Check with your Front Line Command (FLC) to see what is available.

Recruit users across defence

If you have access to MODNET, you can often find staff names and details on your Command Sharepoint. If you do not have access to this or other platforms, ask a permanent staff member to help you.

Do not use social media platforms like Twitter, Instagram, LinkedIn or TikTok to recruit participants for user research.

Army participants

To find research participants in the Army, use the Army Knowledge Exchange (AKX).

Participants with no MODNET access

To find users with little or no access to MODNET, use Defence Connect. You can also use the platform to carry out randomised surveys and questionnaires.

Anonymous research

You can start a discussion or set up a questionnaire on Trusted Voices. MOD staff use aliases on the platform to discuss sensitive topics like pay, mental health and bullying.

Research with disabled users

You must include people with different disabilities in your research. This helps your service meet the needs of all of its users.

You need to include participants who:

  • are disabled or use assistive technologies
  • have limited digital skills or low literacy
  • may need help to use your service

The Defence Disability Network (DDN) can help you recruit participants with condition-specific disabilities.

If you have ideas to improve this topic, contact the team at service-manual@digital.mod.uk.
